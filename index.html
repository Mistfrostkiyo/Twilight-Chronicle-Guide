Introduction

The Basics

Hegemony is a mode where 8 alliances compete for various structures and crystals in order to accumulate AFK rewards, as well as score points that determine your leaderboard rank.

Your alliances initial gameplan should be to scout the opposition and see where they are in relation to your spawn point.

If you see a strong clan to the left of your spawn, make sure to spread away from them and go right.

The first few days will consist of claiming as many crystals as possible, as well as trying to secure space on the map e.g farming basic castles (again it's good to know your opponents).

If you are weaker then prioritize crystal farming.

If you are stronger make paths to get castles).

You should trial and error with teams that can clear the paths.
It is hard to direct accurately as everyone's power in hegemony varies a lot.

For reference, I am 4.7B power and have around 160M power teams at the start of hegemony.

I can clear the outside paths with 2 or 3 weak SSR+ heroes.

By the end of the hegemony week I can clear the outside paths with 1 SSR hero.

When more of the map opens up, and you are a weaker alliance then you can just keep making paths to get crystals.

Crystals become way less of a priority to bigger alliances, especially if 2 big alliances are constantly fighting.

Despite this, if you are a weaker alliance, it is not impossible for you to take cities or castles, which I will explain later on.

The Fatigue System

The fatigue system is very misunderstood, and can be used to MASSIVELY help you in hegemony.

The description in game that talks about fatigue doesn't align with what actually happens, thus there can be some confusion about fatigue.

I will describe it how I've seen it work in game.

When you attack anything in hegemony, your team will gain 5 fatigue stacks (this is the same if you deploy 1 hero to attack, or deploy 6 heroes).

After 5, 10, and 15 attacks, your team will gain a 30%, 60%, and 90% stat reduction respectively.

Alternatively, if your team is in standby mode at any structure on the map, they can also gain fatigue when getting attacked (the same as described above), however (this part I'm not 100% sure about so correct me if needed) I believe that the attacker must attack with a full 6 man team in order to apply the max stacks of fatigue on a defending team e.g you can't spam a 1 hero team into a defending team to fatigue them.

When the defending team gets attacked, then will change from 'standby' mode to 'garrison' mode.

The defending teams first defense against an attacking will consume 10 energy.

The now garrisoning defense will no longer lose energy if they get attacked again, however they will continue to gain fatigue stacks.

To remove the fatigue stacks, you need to leave the base, and re-enter, however this means that if you get attacked again, you will now lose another 10 energy for the initial defense.

Attacking

After understanding how the fatigue system works, you can use it to your advantage to capture structures on the map.

For this scenario, I will keep it simple and pretend the defending team is AFK, all you need to do is break through.

Imagine that there is 1 defending team in the structure that you want to capture, and it has 250M power, but your team is only 100M power.

The way to break through is to use the fatigue system to your advantage.

Firstly, make your 3 teams full of 6 weak heroes (so 18 weak heroes deployed altogether).

Now you will continuously attack the structure. The first 3 attacks will apply 15 fatigue to the defending team.

Once you attack the defending team 5 times, it will now have 30% stat reduction.

So instead of being a 250M power team, it is technically now at 175M power team.

You can keep fatiguing the defense until it is sufficient to defeat with your main team.

This strategy loses a lot of energy for your weak heroes, however allows you to overcome the strong opposition.

This strategy also doesn't rely on anybody else's help from your alliance, so it is good to know if you are in an inactive alliance.

Defending

For this scenario, I will continue from the above scenario.

You managed to capture the structure! However the enemy just woke up and is determined to claim it back.

If you leave you team in the structure to defend, they will just one shot it, then proceed to capture the structure.

So how can you defend it by yourself? Again, take advantage of the fatigue system.

Move your main team 1 tile outside of the structure, preferably directly opposite from where they are attacking from.

After the attacking team starts attacking the structure, they will start to gain fatigue stacks.

So now wait until they have completed either 5, 10, or 15 battles (whatever amount of fatigue is needed for you to defeat them) and then move your defense back into the structure and your team will prevail!

1 of 2 things will happen now :

  • They give up

OR

  • They keep full sending it

Remember though, this one defense cost your team 10 energy, and put them into 'garrison' mode.

To continue to defend, you need to move out, then into the base to heal up, then back out of the base and wait for the attacker to attack.

Creating a Legion

Making use of the legion march feature is one of the most important things for hegemony, HOWEVER it requires an active and competent alliance leader, and requires some coordination with other guild members.

Anybody from your alliance has the ability to create a legion, however you need to be assigned as a city lord of a particular city or castle. Only your alliance leader can assign you to be a city lord, thus the leader himself need to be active.

Once you are a city lord, you need alliance members to find the city/castle in which you are city lord, click on the structure, and press 'proceed to assignment' This will move their teams to your structure, and allow you to form a 'legion conscription'.

You can only select a maximum of 20 teams to join the conscription, however more than 20 teams can be left 'as assignment' in your structure. Select the 20 (or however many amount of teams you desire) teams and create your legion.

Useful information: if there is currently a path to a specific structure you want to attack, you will be able to start your legion march. If during the march the path gets broken, the legion will still continue marching and stop at the tile where the path is broken.

The legion is only able to attack structures on the map, excluding crystals. You cannot use a legion to form paths for you.

I am currently unsure as to how to determine the attack order of your legion, but i can confirm that it is not based from a power scale (at least not from what I've seen) when the legion attacks a structure, all teams attack at once, so if you have 20 teams, you will take out 20 hp from a structure at a time, which allows you to very quickly capture important structures.

Sometimes speed is more useful then energy efficiency.

Having a legion makes farming bosses easy (obviously this is relative to your alliances strength e.g. a weak alliance will still struggle to kill a boss even with a legion).

Fighting For The Center

The final 2 hours can get a bit hectic if you are against other strong alliances that also want to win.

If your team is already winning based on score, then you actually don't need to do anything.

You can apply the defending tactic from above to defend your main structures e.g. the lions, swift stele, adv cities etc.

You do NOT want to attack the genesis idol and 'help' the opposing team claim it.

Instead, the first course of action will be controlling space, and disallowing the enemies to get close to the genesis idol.

If you have a legion available, create a legion march to one of the lions and wait there.

You can't wait 1 tile away from the idol as you can only march to structures, not tiles/crystals.

There is a cheap nut effective tactic that you can use here if your alliance is coordinated.

There are 4 access points to the center, The top and bottom access point has 3 individual tiles and the left and right access point has 2 individual tiles in which teams can enter, so 10 access tiles.

You can leave 10 strong teams sat on these 8 tiles.

What is the purpose of this? well it helps you win the stamina advantage.

This is because let's say your team sat at the access point is 200M power, and the enemy has a 250M power team, the enemy will have to use this strong team to defeat you, and after they defeat you, their strong team will further attack the garrison team on the access tile, resulting in 20 stamina loss for their strong team, and 10 stamina loss for your strong team.

If a breakthrough happens and they start attacking the idol, be prepared to wait outside until the idol is low hp.

If you have a legion close by, and the idol has 20 hp, your legion can be sent into the idol to guarantee the last hit capture.

Defense strategies from earlier apply here again to defend your lead.

If you are currently not first place during the final 2 hours, then it is up to your alliance to make the progressive moves.

In a realistic world, the enemies won't be defending like I mentioned above this (well at least not yet as many people don't know this information yet).

But let's say they are defending perfectly, then you will have to grind slowly to get access to the center.

This includes the tactic mentioned in the 'attacking' section above.

You will have to use your weak teams to fatigue the enemies in the 8 access points and slowly break through.

If the center isn't this heavily defended, then you MUST attack more than 1 thing in the center.

Don't just go for the idol.

Calculate the score difference between you and first place.

A lion gives 1000 score, therefore capturing a lion off of the enemy will close the distance by 2000 (your alliance +1000, enemy alliance -1000 score).

Attacking multiple things can overwhelm the opponents and help you win.

Note, there are situations where it's just not possible to win.

Don't be demoralized, just prioritize on farming as much AFK rewards as possible.
